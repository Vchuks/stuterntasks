Jollof Rice

About Jellof Rice

Jollof rice, is a rice dish from West Africa. The dish is typically made with long-grain rice, tomatoes, onions, spices, vegetables and meat in a single pot, although its ingredients and preparation methods vary across different regions.

The origins of jollof rice can be traced to the Senegambian region that was ruled by the Wolof or Jolof Empire in the 14th century, spanning parts of today's Senegal, The Gambia and Mauritania, where rice was grown. The dish has its roots in a traditional dish called thieboudienne, containing rice, fish, shellfish and vegetables. Food and agriculture historian James C. McCann considers this claim plausible given the popularity of rice in the upper Niger valley, but considers it unlikely that the dish could have spread from Senegal to its current range since such a diffusion is not seen in "linguistic, historical or political patterns". Instead he proposes that the dish spread with the Mali empire, especially the Djula tradespeople who dispersed widely to the regional commercial and urban centers, taking with them economic arts of "blacksmithing, small-scale marketing, and rice agronomy" as well as the religion of Islam.

Ingredients Measurement
Rice 2 cups
Tomatoes A small bowl of grounded tomatoes
Pepper 2 table spoons of grounded pepper
Onions A ball of onions
Groundnut Oil 1/4 litre
Spices 2 tablespoons
Salt 1 tablespoon

Steps/Method of preparation

  1. Parboil the rice
  2. Fry the grinded tomatoes with groundnut oil and onions till the bitter taste is removed.
  3. Into the tomatoe stew, add pepper, spices and salt and leave to boil for 5 minutes.
  4. Pour the rice into the mix
  5. Leave to cook and check at intervals until the water has dried.
  6. Turn off the heat. Bonne Appetit!!
